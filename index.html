Software Development Manager

Having been a Software Developer for more than 15 years, I know that most developers don’t quit jobs - they quit bosses. A team of Software Developers needs a leader, not just in technological skills, but in coordination, organization, and motivation. Being a highly skilled Software Developer speaks volumes to my technical and problem solving expertise, yet I know that to guarantee success as a Software Development Manager, leadership, communication, budgeting, and time management are just as essential. Utilizing Scrum and DevOps practices in an Agile environment, I can ensure my team not only flourishes independently, but that it incorporates itself elegantly within the business as a whole, in unity with all departments necessary to achieve goals and pursue greater heights. When a business is in harmony, employees stick around.

An integral part of being a Software Development Manager is navigating a delicate balance between being a Manager and a Developer. I like to follow the 80-20 rule, representing that 80% of my efforts are focused on management processes and 20% of my time is dedicated to studying the cutting edge of technological advancements in Software Development. This way, I am perpetually a value to my clients as both an expert Software Developer, and a Software Development Manager that can train my team’s technical standards on pace with the ever-evolving, fast-paced world of development. I’ve managed a diverse range of Software Developers throughout my career and my success has been in knowing that all of these skills I possess need to integrate seamlessly in order to orchestrate a happy and productive team, consistently driving the accelerated success of the respective business.
